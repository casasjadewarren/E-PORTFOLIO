E-PORTFOLIO

THE TEACHER AND THE SCHOOL CURRICULUM

E-PORTFOLIO OVERVIEW

The E-Portfolio is a compilation of students' synthesis, application, and reflection outputs per unit. It documents conceptual understanding, educational applications, professional insights, and personal growth throughout the course.

COURSE DISCRIPTION

PED 2208: The Teacher and the School Curriculum focuses on the nature, foundations, phases, processes, implementation, evaluation, and current trends in curriculum development. The course emphasizes the role of teachers as curriculum planners, implementers, evaluators, and innovators who respond to the changing needs of learners and society.

GENERAL INTENDED LEARNING

At the end of the course, the students should be able to:

1. Explain the concepts, purposes, and types of curriculum.

2. Analyze the philosophical, psychological, socio-historical, and legal foundations of curriculum

3. Examine the phases and processes of curriculum development.

4. Demonstrate understanding of curriculum planning, design, implementation, and evaluation.

5. Critically analyze current trends and issues in curriculum development.

6. Apply curriculum concepts in designing meaningful learning experiences.

7. Develop reflective, analytical, and professional insights regarding curriculum and instruction.

8. Prepare curriculum-aligned and learner-centered lesson plans.

9. Appreciate the teacher's role as an active participant in curriculum development and improvement.
