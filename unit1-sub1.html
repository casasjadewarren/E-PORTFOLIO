Unit 1 - Synthesis

1. Explain the curriculum in your own understanding.

A school cannot truly exist without a curriculum because the curriculum serves as the foundation and guide of education. It provides direction on what students should learn, how teachers should teach, and what goals the institution aims to achieve. Without a curriculum, teaching would become disorganized, lessons would lack purpose, and students might not develop the necessary knowledge, skills, and values systematically. It also serves as the basis for assessment and evaluation to determine whether learning objectives are being achieved. Moreover, a curriculum is not only limited to subjects and textbooks; it also includes learning experiences, teaching methods, and the overall development of students. Even informal or alternative schools follow some form of curriculum, whether written or unwritten, because education always requires structure and direction. Therefore, the curriculum is considered the heart or blueprint of a school since it shapes and guides theentire educational process. Curriculum is planned and guided by the teachers. It happens inside and outside the classroom; As a student of Iloilo State University of Fisheries Science and Technology under the department of education, the curriculum can be experienced both inside and outside the classroom. Inside the classroom, the curriculum is reflected through subjects such as grammar, literature, communication, and teaching strategies, where instructors provide lessons, discussions, activities, and assessments that help students develop knowledge and skills in English education. For example, classroom reporting, lesson demonstrations, essay writing, and oral presentations are part of the curriculum because they are planned learning experiences designed to prepare BSED English students to become future educators. Outside the classroom, the curriculum is also evident through activities and experiences that contribute to students’ growth and development. In ISUFST-SEC, this may include participation in school organizations, literary events, seminars, outreach programs, practice teaching preparations, and school programs where students enhance their communication, leadership, and social skills. For instance, joining an English-related organization (Igang publication), participating in declamation or essay-writing contests, or attending educational seminars helps students apply what they learned in class to real-life situations. These experiences form part of the curriculum because they continue to shape students academically, socially, and professionally beyond the four walls of the classroom.

2. Differentiate the different types of curriculum.

The different types of curriculum have different functions in education. The recommended curriculum refers to the curriculum proposed by educational experts, government agencies, and professional organizations to improve learning standards. The written curriculum is the official and documented curriculum found in syllabi, modules, lesson plans, and curriculum guides prepared by schools and teachers. The taught curriculum is the actual implementation of lessons and activities conducted by teachers inside the classroom. The supported curriculum includes the materials and resources used to support teaching and learning such as books, laboratories, technology, and facilities. The assessed curriculum focuses on the evaluation of students through quizzes, examinations, projects, and performance tasks to measure learning outcomes. The learned curriculum refers to the knowledge, skills, attitudes, and values that students truly acquire from their educational experiences. Lastly, the hidden curriculum consists of the lessons and values students learn indirectly from school culture, teacher behavior, discipline, and social interactions. These different types of curriculum work together to make the educational process meaningful and effective.

3. Purpose of Curriculum in Education

The purpose of curriculum in education is to provide direction and organization in the teaching and learning process. It ensures that students acquire the necessary knowledge, skills, values, and competencies needed for personal growth and societal development. Curriculum helps teachers identify learning goals and appropriate teaching strategies. It also promotes consistency in education by ensuring that learners receive quality instruction based on established standards. Furthermore, curriculum prepares students for future careers, responsible citizenship, and lifelong learning. It serves as a bridge between educational objectives and real-life application. Through curriculum, schools are able to develop learners holistically by addressing intellectual, emotional, social, and moral development.

4. Importance of Curriculum in Education

Curriculum is important in education because it serves as the heart of the learning process. It guides teachers on what topics to teach and helps students understand what they are expected to learn. A well-designed curriculum promotes effective teaching and meaningful learning experiences. It also ensures that education remains relevant to the needs of society and the changing world. Curriculum helps develop critical thinking, communication skills, creativity, and problem-solving abilities among learners. In addition, it provides equal learning opportunities for students by following educational standards. Without curriculum, teaching would lack direction and organization. Therefore, curriculum is important because it shapes the quality of education and the future of learners.

5. Explain the process of curriculum development.

The process of curriculum development involves several important steps to ensure effective learning. The first step is identifying the educational needs of learners and society. After identifying the needs, curriculum planners set clear goals and objectives that students are expected to achieve. The next step is selecting and organizing the content, learning experiences, and teaching methods appropriate for the learners. Instructional materials and assessment tools are also prepared to support the curriculum. Once the curriculum is designed, it is implemented by teachers in the classroom. After implementation, evaluation is conducted to determine whether the curriculum objectives are successfully achieved. Feedback from teachers, students, and stakeholders is gathered to identify strengths and areas for improvement. Finally, revisions and adjustments are made to improve the curriculum and make it more responsive to learners’ needs.

6. Discuss the role of teachers and stakeholders in curriculum development.

As a pre-service teacher, I believe that teachers and stakeholders play a very important role in curriculum development. Teachers are directly involved in implementing the curriculum inside the classroom, so their experiences and observations are valuable in improving educational programs. They help identify the strengths and weaknesses of the curriculum based on students’ performance and participation. Teachers also adapt teaching strategies and learning activities to meet the diverse needs of learners. As a future educator, I understand that I must become flexible, creative, and responsible in delivering the curriculum effectively. Stakeholders such as parents, school administrators, community leaders, and government agencies also contribute to curriculum development by providing support, suggestions, and resources. Parents help reinforce learning at home and communicate the needs of students. School administrators ensure that teachers receive proper training and instructional materials. The community and industries can also provide insights about the skills and values needed in real-life situations. Government agencies establish educational standards and policies that guide curriculum implementation. Through collaboration among teachers and stakeholders, the curriculum becomes more responsive, relevant, and effective for learners. I believe that cooperation and shared responsibility are important in achieving quality education for every student.
