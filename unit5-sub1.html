1. Summarize your major learnings from PED 2208.

From PED 2208, I learned that curriculum is not just a list of subjects but a complete guide that shapes teaching and learning in education. I gained understanding about the different types of curriculum such as written, taught, learned, and hidden curriculum, and how each one affects students’ learning experiences. I also learned about the philosophical, psychological, social, and legal foundations of curriculum that guide its development. As a pre-service teacher, I realized that effective teaching requires careful planning, implementation, and evaluation of the curriculum. Overall, this course helped me understand that education is a continuous process of improvement and adaptation to learners’ needs.

2. Explain how the course deepened your understanding of curriculum and instruction.

This course deepened my understanding of curriculum and instruction by showing me how learning experiences are carefully planned and delivered in the classroom. I learned that curriculum is not only about what is taught but also how it is taught and how students learn from it. It also helped me understand the important role of teachers in adapting lessons to meet the needs of different learners. As a student at Iloilo State University of Fisheries Science and Technology taking up Bachelor of Secondary Education major in English, I realized that instruction must always be aligned with the curriculum goals. This course made me more aware that effective instruction requires creativity, flexibility, and continuous reflection.

3. Discuss the importance of curriculum in effective teaching.

Curriculum is important in effective teaching because it serves as the foundation and guide for what teachers should teach and what students should learn. It ensures that lessons are organized, structured, and aligned with educational goals and standards. Without a curriculum, teaching would lack direction and consistency, making it difficult for students to achieve learning outcomes. It also helps teachers choose appropriate teaching strategies, materials, and assessments that fit the needs of learners. As a result, curriculum plays a key role in making teaching more effective, meaningful, and successful in developing students’ knowledge, skills, and values.
