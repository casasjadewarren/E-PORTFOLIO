Unit 2 - Synthesis

1. Compare the major philosophical foundations of curriculum.

The major philosophical foundations of curriculum include Perennialism, Essentialism, Progressivism, and Reconstructionism. Perennialism focuses on teaching timeless knowledge and classic ideas that remain important through the years. Essentialism emphasizes basic skills and subjects like Math, Science, and Language that students need in everyday life. Progressivism believes that students learn best through experience, activities, and problem-solving. Reconstructionism encourages education to help improve society and solve social issues. These philosophies guide teachers in choosing teaching methods, learning activities, and lesson content for students.

2. Explain how psychology contributes to curriculum development.

Psychology contributes to curriculum development by helping educators understand how students think, learn, and behave. It helps teachers create lessons that match the age, interests, abilities, and learning styles of students. Psychology also helps improve teaching strategies to make learning easier and more enjoyable. For example, teachers use motivation, group activities, and interactive discussions to help students participate better in class. It also helps schools support students who may have difficulties in learning or focusing. Because of psychology, curriculum becomes more student-centered and effective.

3. Discuss how society, culture, and history influence curriculum.

Society, culture, and history greatly influence curriculum because education reflects the beliefs, traditions, and needs of people. Society affects curriculum by determining the skills and knowledge students need for the future. Culture influences the values, language, traditions, and practices taught in schools. History also helps students learn about important events, heroes, and lessons from the past. For example, Philippine history and culture are included in the curriculum to help students appreciate their identity as Filipinos. Through these influences, curriculum becomes more meaningful and connected to real life.

4. Explain the importance of legal foundations and educational policies.

Legal foundations and educational policies are important because they serve as the rules and guidelines for education. They help ensure that schools provide quality and equal learning opportunities for all students. Laws and policies also protect the rights of learners and teachers inside the school. In the Philippines, the 1987 Philippine Constitution supports every Filipino’s right to education. Educational policies also guide schools in implementing subjects, grading systems, and teaching standards. Because of these legal foundations, the education system becomes organized and fair.

5. Discuss how the curriculum reflects societal needs and educational goals.

The curriculum reflects societal needs and educational goals because it is designed to prepare students for real-life situations and future careers. Schools teach skills and values that society considers important, such as communication, critical thinking, respect, and responsibility. Curriculum also changes over time to match the needs of modern society and technology. For example, subjects related to digital literacy and environmental awareness are now included in education. Educational goals aim to develop students into productive, knowledgeable, and responsible citizens. Through curriculum, schools help students become prepared for both work and life in society.
