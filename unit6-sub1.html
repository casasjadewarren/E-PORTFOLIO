Unit 6 - Synthesis

1. How did this course shape your perspective as a future teacher?

This course shaped my perspective as a future teacher by helping me realize that teaching is not just about delivering lessons but about understanding how curriculum guides the entire learning process. I learned that a teacher plays a very important role in planning, implementing, and evaluating lessons to meet the needs of learners. It also made me understand that education should be flexible and responsive to students’ abilities and real-life situations. As a pre-service teacher, I realized that I need to be more patient, reflective, and creative in handling different types of learners. Overall, this course helped me see teaching as a continuous process of growth and responsibility.

2. Which unit impacted you the most and why?

The unit that impacted me the most is Unit 1: Curriculum Development. This unit helped me understand how curriculum is carefully planned, designed, and improved to meet the needs of learners and society. It also taught me that curriculum is not fixed because it changes based on educational goals, research, and societal demands. I realized that teachers are not only implementers but also contributors to curriculum development. This unit was meaningful to me because it gave me a strong foundation in understanding how education systems work.

3. How will you apply your learnings from PED 2208 in your future teaching practice?

I will apply my learnings from PED 2208 in my future teaching practice by carefully planning lessons that are aligned with curriculum goals and the needs of my students. I will use different teaching strategies that make learning more engaging, meaningful, and student-centered. I will also consider the different types of curriculum, especially the hidden and learned curriculum, to ensure positive learning experiences inside and outside the classroom. As a future teacher, I will reflect on my teaching practices and continuously improve through evaluation and feedback. I will also strive to become more flexible and understanding, especially for learners who have different learning styles and attention challenges.
